MRG TRIPLES WAREHOUSE SPACE IN ANTICIPATION OF CONTINUED
RAPID GROWTH

COLORADO SPRINGS, Colo.--Oct. 16, 2001--Military Resale
Group, Inc. (OTCBB: BCTL) is pleased to announce it has
moved from its previous 12,000-square-foot warehouse to one
containing almost 33,000 square feet.

This move will allow the Company to approximately triple
its dry space, double its cooler space and triple its
freezer space. Ethan D. Hokit, President, stated that
``many household name companies have requested that we
begin to service their accounts in our current market.
Consequently, we continue our planned multiphase expansion
to serve the Military Resale Market. Military Resale Group,
Inc. is negotiating with several companies to obtain new
business and anticipates showing continued growth in sales,
which the new warehouse would accommodate.''

In 1999, Military Resale Group, Inc. was housed in a 2,500-
square-foot warehouse and a few months later acquired
another 2,000 square feet. In October 1999, the Company
acquired another 7,500 square feet, bringing its total to
12,000 square feet. This space was adequate until a few
months ago, when it became evident that more warehouse
space was needed to support the growth that the Company is
experiencing.

Military Resale Group, Inc. (MRG) was incorporated in 1997
when it acquired a 50-year-old distributor to the Military
Resale Market. At that time MRG's annual sales were
$750,000, and they have since soared five-fold in three
years to $4.5 million in 2000. Military Resale Group is now
a publicly owned company as a result of its recent
acquisition by Bactrol Technologies, Inc. (OTCBB: BCTL).

The Military Resale Market is a $15 billion niche market,
with 13,000,000 authorized patrons, which operates under
the aegis of the Defense Commissary Agency (DeCA), a
Department of Defense agency. The DOD's objective, in this
area, is to operate resale store systems, which sell
groceries (at commissaries) and general merchandise (at
exchanges, i.e., PXs) at the lowest possible prices. The
resulting average cost savings of about 25% are a major
fringe benefit (untaxed) to military personnel. The
competitive structure of the Military Resale Market
consists of a few large distributors, who do not dominate
the industry, and hundreds of small ones. The post-
acquisition strategy of MRG is to grow, primarily, by
acquiring other small distributors and, secondarily, by
internal growth.
