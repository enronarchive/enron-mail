What's in store
Investors get ready to ride into smoother waters

By Carla Drysdale, columnist

As we bid farewell to a year in which many of us dealt
directly with loss of lives, jobs, and investments, the
Big Ball of 2002 is poised to bring in what we hope will
be a brighter new year. Now, more than halfway
through the fourth quarter, the venture capital market
is still far from robust. Where will it go from here and
which will be the startups that drive VC investments in
2002?

For the moment, venture capitalists are putting most
of their money into saving existing businesses, rather
than starting new ones. According to data from
VentureWire, a division of New York-based
Technologic Partners, halfway through the fourth
quarter, seed money and first-round financing
accounted for just 12 percent of all disbursements,
down from 25 percent in the first quarter.
